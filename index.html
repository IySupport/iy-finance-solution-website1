FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often. Still unsure? Our consultants are a phone call away.

What is debt counselling (debt review)? +

Debt counselling is a formal process under the National Credit Act that helps over-indebted South Africans restructure their debt repayments into one affordable monthly instalment, while protecting you from legal action by credit providers.

Will I lose my house or car under debt review? +

No — debt review is specifically designed to protect your assets. As long as you keep to your restructured repayment plan, your home and vehicle remain protected while you repay your debt at a rate you can afford.

How long does the debt review process take? +

The initial restructuring is typically finalised within about 60 business days. How long you remain under debt review depends on your debt and repayment plan — your consultant will give you a realistic timeline based on your specific circumstances.

How does credit clearance work? +

We assess your credit report and assist with the removal of listings that legally qualify — such as paid-up judgments, expired defaults and completed debt review flags. Timelines vary per case, and we'll advise you honestly on what can and cannot be removed.

What does funeral cover include? +

Our funeral cover is tailored to your family's needs and budget, covering you and your loved ones with a payout that helps with funeral costs when it matters most. Speak to a consultant for a custom quote.

How much does a consultation cost? +

Your first consultation is free and completely confidential. We'll assess your situation, explain your options and recommend the most suitable solution — with no obligation to proceed.
